### [36] [Receiver-Aware Analysis and Verification of the Spectral Separation Coefficient Under Interference-Induced Degradation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18196)
*Lucas Heublein,Fabian Benschuh,Alexander Rügamer,Felix Ott*

Main category: eess.SP

TL;DR: 本文通过显式纳入接收机前端特性，计算了接收机依赖的频谱分离系数（SSC）和干扰影响，并在真实开放场数据集（210种干扰场景）和射频星座模拟器控制的测试环境中进行了实验验证，证明了干扰影响计算的鲁棒性。

Motivation: 现有干扰效应评估通常使用接收机无关的度量，接收机前端特性要么被理想化处理，要么仅隐含考虑，导致评估不准确。本文旨在通过显式纳入接收机特定前端特性，解决这一局限性。

Method: 录制包含210种不同干扰场景的真实开放场数据集，计算特定接收机模块的接收机依赖型频谱分离系数（SSC）和干扰影响；并使用射频星座模拟器（RFCS）生成受控数据集进行验证。

Conclusion: 通过明确考虑接收机前端特性，可以实现更准确的干扰效应量化，且该方法在真实和受控环境下均表现鲁棒。

Abstract: Interference poses a significant challenge to satellite-based positioning systems, making it essential to accurately quantify the effects of specific interference types on receiver performance and the resulting reliability of position computation. In current practice, interference effects are often quantified using receiver-independent metrics, with receiver-specific front-end characteristics either idealized or only implicitly considered. In this paper, we address this limitation by explicitly incorporating receiver-specific front-end characteristics into the computation of interference effects and validating the resulting receiver-dependent analysis experimentally. Therefore, we record a real-world open-field dataset comprising 210 distinct interference scenarios and compute the receiver-dependent spectral separation coefficient (SSC) and interference impact for a specific receiver module. Furthermore, we verify the computation using a controlled dataset generated with a radio frequency constellation simulator (RFCS), employing the same receiver module and replaying similar interferences classes. The comparison of results obtained in both environments demonstrates the robustness of the interference impact computation.
